How They Trapped You

A scheduling tool shows your available slots. Someone clicks one. It creates a calendar event and sends a confirmation. That's it. That's the entire product.

Calendly's free plan: one meeting type, basic availability. But round-robin for a team? Pay. Remove their branding? Pay. Custom questions? Pay. Automated reminders? Pay. What started as "free" becomes $16/month, then $20 with a team member, then more with workflows. Endless ladder.

The Replacements

Cal.com is open-source, looks almost identical to Calendly, and gives you everything for free: unlimited event types, unlimited calendars, custom branding, round-robin, workflow automation. Self-host or use their cloud — free for features Calendly charges $16+ for.

TidyCal charges $29 — once. Not per month. Once in your life. Every feature included. No upsells. Connects to Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, every payment processor.

Calendly $16/mo = $192/year. TidyCal $29 lifetime. By month three, TidyCal paid for itself. By year five: $951 saved — for the same product.

Why Each One Works

Airtable → NocoDB. NocoDB is an open-source Airtable alternative that turns any MySQL/PostgreSQL database into a smart spreadsheet. Same grid view, same Kanban boards, same forms, same API. Airtable charges $24/month for the privilege of storing your own data on their servers. NocoDB lets you store it on yours — for free. The interface is nearly identical. Your team won't notice the difference.

Loom → Screenity. Loom charges $15/month to record your screen and share a link. Screenity is a free, open-source Chrome extension that does the exact same thing: screen recording, webcam overlay, microphone, instant shareable link. No account required. No watermarks. No storage limits. It's a browser extension — install it in 10 seconds and you're done. Loom's entire business model is "we put a record button in a nice UI and charge you $15/month for it." Screenity proves you don't need the UI.

Notion AI → Cursor. Notion's AI add-on is $10/month for AI-assisted writing inside your docs. Cursor is a free code editor (VS Code fork) with AI built in that's dramatically more powerful — and if you just need AI writing, you can use the free tier of Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini directly. You're paying $10/month to have a slightly worse AI trapped inside a note-taking app. Use a real AI tool for free instead.

Dubsado → n8n + Brevo. This is the big one. Dubsado charges $40/month for CRM, invoicing, scheduling, and email — an "all-in-one" platform. But you already replaced the scheduling (Cal.com), the email (Brevo), and the automation (n8n). All that's left is the CRM and invoicing — and free tools like n8n + Google Sheets handle both. Dubsado's $40/month is paying for integration you can build yourself in an afternoon with the free stack you already set up.
